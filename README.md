# student-performance-analysis
This data mining project analyzes how various socioeconomic factors impact student academic performance. The study investigates the relationship between students' social and economic backgrounds and their scores in math, reading, and writing examinations. Using a dataset of student records, the project builds predictive models to identify key factors that influence academic success and provides insights for educational interventions.

Parental Education Impact: Students with parents holding higher education degrees (Master's, Bachelor's) consistently outperformed those with parents having only high school education by 15-20% in average scores.

Economic Factor Significance: Students with standard lunch scored approximately 12% higher across all subjects compared to those with free/reduced lunch, highlighting the importance of nutrition in academic performance.

Test Preparation Effectiveness: Students who completed test preparation courses showed a 10-15% improvement in pass rates, demonstrating the value of structured study support.

Performance Patterns:
Math scores showed the strongest correlation with socioeconomic factors.
Reading and writing scores were more closely correlated with each other.
Female students generally outperformed male students in reading and writing.
